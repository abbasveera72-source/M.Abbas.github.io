# Why I Created a GitHub Portfolio Website as a Computer Engineering Student

As a second semester Computer Engineering student at **UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus**, this semester introduced me to many new ideas that extended beyond traditional classroom learning. Among all the activities and assignments, one idea particularly changed the way I think about my future as an engineer: creating a personal portfolio website on GitHub.

The idea of creating a portfolio website was introduced to our class by **Dr. Bilal Ahmad**. Throughout the semester, Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to think beyond grades and examinations. He repeatedly emphasized that students should leave a positive digital footprint and build an online presence that reflects their skills, projects, and learning journey.

At first, I was excited because the concept sounded modern and professional. I had already heard about GitHub, but I had never seriously thought about using it as a platform to showcase my work and experiences.

In today's digital world, having an online presence is becoming increasingly important for engineering students and software professionals. Recruiters, internship coordinators, and employers often search candidates online before making hiring decisions.

When Dr. Bilal Ahmad first discussed the importance of digital footprints, I immediately became interested in the idea. His approach to teaching has always focused on practical applications of technology. In Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems, he consistently encouraged us to apply theoretical concepts to real-world scenarios.

I created my portfolio website using GitHub Pages. Developing the website was an exciting experience because it allowed me to apply concepts that I had only recently started learning.

One of the biggest difficulties I faced was working with HTML. Although HTML appears simple initially, designing a clean and organized portfolio requires careful attention to detail. Small mistakes in tags, formatting, or page structure sometimes caused unexpected issues.

Another major challenge involved deployment. Publishing the website using GitHub Pages was not as straightforward as I initially expected. There were moments when the website did not load correctly or changes did not appear after updating the repository.

Despite these challenges, the excitement of seeing my own website live on the internet was truly rewarding. For the first time, I felt that I had created something publicly accessible that represented me as a Computer Engineering student.

This project also changed my perspective regarding learning. Previously, I mainly focused on completing assignments for grades. However, building a portfolio website demonstrated that academic activities can contribute directly to long-term professional growth.

For this reason, I have decided that I will continue updating my portfolio website even after this semester ends. I want my website to document my entire engineering journey, including future academic projects, programming experiences, and personal reflections.

Looking back, I am grateful that Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to undertake this project. What initially appeared to be a simple classroom activity became a meaningful step toward establishing my professional identity.

This portfolio website represents more than just a collection of webpages. It symbolizes the beginning of my digital footprint, my commitment to continuous learning, and my ambition to grow as a future Computer Engineer.
