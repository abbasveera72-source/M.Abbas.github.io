# My Digital Footprint Journey: Building My First GitHub Portfolio Website

As a second semester Computer Engineering student at **UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus**, this semester introduced me to many ideas that extended beyond traditional classroom learning. Among all the assignments and activities, one idea particularly changed the way I think about my future as an engineer: creating a personal portfolio website on GitHub.

The idea of creating a portfolio website was introduced to our class by **Dr. Bilal Ahmad**. Throughout the semester, Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to think beyond grades and examinations. He repeatedly emphasized that students should leave a positive digital footprint and build an online presence that reflects their skills, projects, and learning journey.

At first, I was excited because the concept sounded modern and professional. I had already heard about GitHub, but I had never seriously thought about using it as a platform to showcase my work and experiences.

In today's digital world, having an online presence is becoming increasingly important for students and professionals. Recruiters, internship coordinators, and employers often search candidates online before making hiring decisions. A portfolio website allows students to showcase their skills, projects, and achievements in a structured and professional manner.

I created my portfolio website using **GitHub Pages**. Developing the website was an exciting experience because it allowed me to apply concepts that I had only recently started learning.

One of the biggest difficulties I faced was working with **HTML**. Although HTML appears simple initially, designing a clean and organized portfolio requires careful attention to detail. Small mistakes in tags, formatting, or page structure sometimes caused unexpected issues. Debugging these problems taught me patience and improved my problem-solving skills.

Another major challenge involved **deployment**. Publishing the website using GitHub Pages was not as straightforward as I initially expected. There were moments when the website did not load correctly or changes did not appear after updating the repository. I had to explore GitHub settings, understand repository configurations, and learn how deployment works.

Despite these challenges, the excitement of seeing my own website live on the internet was truly rewarding. For the first time, I felt that I had created something publicly accessible that represented me as a Computer Engineering student.

This project also changed my perspective regarding learning. Previously, I mainly focused on completing assignments for grades. However, building a portfolio website demonstrated that academic activities can contribute directly to long-term professional growth. Every project, assignment, and technical experience can become part of a student's digital portfolio.

Another important lesson I learned is the importance of consistency. A portfolio website should not remain static after completion. Instead, it should continuously evolve. New projects, technical blogs, achievements, certifications, and reflections can be added regularly.

For this reason, I have decided that I will continue updating my portfolio website even after this semester ends. I want my website to document my entire engineering journey, including future academic projects, programming experiences, and personal reflections.

Looking back, I am grateful that Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to undertake this project. What initially appeared to be a simple classroom activity became a meaningful step toward establishing my professional identity.

This portfolio website represents more than just a collection of webpages. It symbolizes the beginning of my digital footprint, my commitment to continuous learning, and my ambition to grow as a future Computer Engineer. I am excited to continue this journey and document every important milestone along the way.
