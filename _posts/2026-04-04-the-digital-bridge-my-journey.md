My story doesn't begin in a high-tech lab or in front of a glowing monitor. It begins with the simple, grounded life of a **"Chenabian."** Growing up near the banks of the Chenab, life had a different rhythm. It was a world defined by the seasons, by the vastness of the fields, and by a community where everyone knew your name.

However, even then, I found myself fascinated by how things worked. Whether it was the mechanics of a tractor in the field or the way electricity transformed a dark room at night, I was always asking "Why?" That curiosity is the seed of every engineer. I learned the value of hard work and resilience from the people around me.

<div style="clear: both;"></div>

## The Transition: Entering UET Lahore

Leaving the familiar comfort of my hometown to join the **University of Engineering and Technology (UET), Lahore**, was a turning point. Stepping onto the campus was both a proud moment and a humbling challenge. Suddenly, I was a **Computer Engineering** student (Roll No. 149).

  <img src="{{ '/assets/images/picture-2.png' | relative_url }}" alt="UET Lahore Computer Engineering" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 6px; display: block;">
  <p style="margin: 5px 0 0; text-align: center; color: #888; font-size: 0.8rem; font-style: italic;">The new frontier: UET Lahore.</p>

Life at UET is a whirlwind of 16-credit hour semesters and late-night lab reports. It was here that I discovered my passion for **Machine Learning** and **Data Intelligence**. I realized that the same logic I used to understand the world back home could be applied to complex algorithms and SQL databases.
