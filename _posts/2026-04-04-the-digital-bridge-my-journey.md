  <h2 style="color: #1a1a1a; border: none; margin-top: 30px;">The Roots: Life as a Chenabian</h2>
  
  <div style="float: left; width: 300px; margin: 0 20px 10px 0; padding: 5px; border: 1px solid #e1e4e8; border-radius: 8px; background: white; box-shadow: 0 4px 6px rgba(0,0,0,0.05);">
    <img src="{{ '/assets/images/chenab_roots.jpg' | relative_url }}" alt="Scenic view of the Chenab region" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 6px; object-fit: cover;">
    <p style="margin: 5px 0 0; text-align: center; color: #888; font-size: 0.8rem; font-style: italic;">Where curiosity began: Near the banks of the Chenab.</p>
  </div>

  <p>
    My story doesn't begin in a high-tech lab or in front of a glowing monitor. It begins with the simple, grounded life of a "Chenabian." Growing up near the banks of the Chenab, life had a different rhythm. It was a world defined by the seasons, by the vastness of the fields, and by a community where everyone knew your name. In those early days, technology was a distant concept, something found in textbooks rather than in our daily pockets.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    However, even then, I found myself fascinated by how things worked. Whether it was the mechanics of a tractor in the field or the way electricity transformed a dark room at night, I was always asking "Why?" That curiosity is the seed of every engineer. I learned the value of hard work and resilience from the people around me—lessons that no university lecture can fully replicate.
  </p>
  
  <p style="clear: both;">
    This foundational experience instilled in me a practical understanding of systems. If a water pump failed, it required structured troubleshooting—identifying the problem, proposing a fix, and testing it. In many ways, that was my first introduction to the algorithmic logic I would later apply to Computer Engineering.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #1a1a1a; border: none; margin-top: 40px;">The Transition: Entering UET Lahore</h2>
  
  <p>
    Leaving the familiar comfort of my hometown to join the **University of Engineering and Technology (UET), Lahore**, was a turning point. Stepping onto the campus of one of Pakistan’s most prestigious institutions was both a proud moment and a humbling challenge. Suddenly, I wasn't just a student; I was a **Computer Engineering** student (Roll No. 149), tasked with mastering the invisible languages that power the modern world.
  </p>
  
  <div style="float: right; width: 300px; margin: 10px 0 10px 20px; padding: 5px; border: 1px solid #e1e4e8; border-radius: 8px; background: white; box-shadow: 0 4px 6px rgba(0,0,0,0.05);">
    <img src="{{ '/assets/images/uet_department.jpg' | relative_url }}" alt="UET Lahore Computer Engineering Department" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 6px; object-fit: cover;">
    <p style="margin: 5px 0 0; text-align: center; color: #888; font-size: 0.8rem; font-style: italic;">The new frontier: UET Lahore, Computer Engineering.</p>
  </div>

  <p>
    Life at UET is a whirlwind of 16-credit hour semesters, late-night lab reports, and the constant hum of brainstorming sessions in the department. It was here that I discovered my passion for **Machine Learning** and **Data Intelligence**. I realized that the same logic I used to understand the world back home could be applied to complex algorithms and SQL databases.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    The transition was not just academic; it was cultural. UET Lahore is a melting pot of talent from across Pakistan. The collaborative environment and the challenge to innovate have driven me to push beyond my limits. I learned that the true essence of engineering is not just in mastering technical skills, but in applying them creatively to solve societal problems.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #1a1a1a; border: none; margin-top: 40px;">The Balance: Logic and Life</h2>
  
  <p style="clear: both;">
    General life as an engineering student is a balancing act. On one hand, my days are filled with Python scripts, predictive modeling, and survival analysis research. I spend hours debugging code and ensuring that my SQL queries are optimized. But on the other hand, I remain a Chenabian at heart. I value the quiet moments of reflection, the conversations with friends over tea at the university cafeteria, and the responsibility of representing my family and my roots in everything I build.
  </p>

  <div style="background: #f8f9fa; padding: 30px; border-radius: 15px; border-top: 5px solid #007bff; margin: 40px 0; text-align: left; box-shadow: inset 0 2px 5px rgba(0,0,0,0.02);">
    <h3 style="margin-top: 0; color: #1a1a1a;">Looking Forward</h3>
    <p>
      As I look toward the future, my goal is to use the technical skills I am gaining at UET to solve real-world problems. Whether it is improving healthcare through breast cancer analysis or building more efficient IoT architectures, I want my work to have a human impact.
    </p>
    <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
      My life is a story of two worlds: the traditional values of my past and the digital possibilities of my future. Through **M.Abbas Tech**, I hope to document this journey—not just as an engineer, but as a person striving to make sense of a rapidly changing world.
    </p>
  </div>
